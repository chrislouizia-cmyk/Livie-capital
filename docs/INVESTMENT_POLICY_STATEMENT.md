# LIVIE CAPITAL INVESTMENT POLICY STATEMENT

## Purpose

This Investment Policy Statement defines the portfolio management standards, risk controls, and reporting discipline of Livie Capital. The policy is designed to support the firm's founding objective: protect capital, compound consistently, and grow responsibly over the long term.

## Portfolio Construction Rules

Livie Capital portfolios shall be constructed with a diversified allocation across liquid public market instruments and approved strategic exposures. Portfolio construction must prioritize capital preservation, liquidity, transparency, and risk-adjusted return potential.

Eligible portfolio exposures include:

- S&P 500 ETFs
- Nasdaq ETFs
- Technology equities
- Value equities
- Dividend equities
- Gold exposure through XAUUSD or equivalent instruments
- GBPUSD foreign exchange exposure
- Cash and cash-equivalent reserves

No allocation should be made solely on return potential. Each allocation must be assessed against liquidity, volatility, downside risk, correlation to existing holdings, and contribution to total portfolio risk.

## Position Sizing Rules

Position sizing shall be determined by risk budget, not conviction alone. The maximum risk per trade is 0.80% of portfolio equity.

Each position must define:

- Entry price
- Stop loss
- Take profit or review target
- Expected holding period
- Risk per trade
- Rationale for inclusion

Position sizes should be reduced when volatility rises, correlations increase, or portfolio drawdown approaches defined risk thresholds.

## Rebalancing Rules

Portfolio allocations shall be reviewed regularly to ensure exposures remain consistent with mandate, risk tolerance, and market conditions.

Rebalancing may be triggered by:

- Material deviation from target allocation
- Significant market volatility
- Change in risk-adjusted opportunity
- Breach of portfolio concentration limits
- Drawdown or liquidity concerns
- Strategic review following underperformance

Rebalancing should be executed with attention to transaction costs, tax implications where applicable, liquidity conditions, and execution risk.

## Risk Management Process

Risk management is the primary operating discipline of Livie Capital. The firm seeks to avoid permanent capital impairment and maintain consistency across market cycles.

The risk management process includes:

- Pre-trade risk assessment
- Defined stop loss before entry
- Ongoing exposure monitoring
- Portfolio drawdown monitoring
- Position-level risk review
- Correlation and concentration review
- Liquidity assessment
- Scenario analysis during stressed conditions

Risk decisions must be documented and reviewed when portfolio conditions materially change.

## Forex Exposure Policy

Foreign exchange exposure may be used for diversification, hedging, and tactical opportunity. GBPUSD is an approved foreign exchange exposure under the current investment universe.

Forex positions must be sized conservatively due to leverage, volatility, and macro sensitivity. Currency exposure should not dominate portfolio risk unless explicitly approved as part of a documented strategy.

Forex exposure must be monitored for:

- Leverage impact
- Interest rate sensitivity
- Macro event risk
- Correlation with equity and commodity exposures
- Contribution to total portfolio volatility

## Drawdown Response Plan

Livie Capital maintains a maximum portfolio drawdown threshold of 20%. A mandatory strategy review is required if the portfolio declines 15% from its equity peak.

Drawdown response framework:

1. At 5% drawdown: review active positions and confirm original thesis.
2. At 10% drawdown: reduce non-core risk and reassess portfolio correlations.
3. At 15% drawdown: conduct mandatory strategy review and suspend discretionary risk increases.
4. At 20% drawdown: suspend new positions and prioritize capital preservation until risk conditions normalize.

If the maximum drawdown threshold is reached, new positions shall be suspended until the portfolio has been reviewed and a formal recovery plan has been established.

## Reporting Methodology

Livie Capital shall maintain reporting standards suitable for institutional review and internal discipline.

Reports should include:

- Net asset value
- Daily, weekly, monthly, and annual performance
- Open positions
- Closed trades
- Unrealized and realized profit/loss
- Risk per trade
- Portfolio exposure
- Drawdown status
- Allocation summary
- Commentary on material portfolio decisions

Reporting should be timely, consistent, and transparent. Performance and risk figures should be calculated using a repeatable methodology.

## Performance Measurement Standards

Performance shall be measured on a net basis where possible and evaluated against both absolute return objectives and risk-adjusted standards.

Primary performance standards include:

- Total return
- Annualized return
- Net asset value growth
- Maximum drawdown
- Sharpe ratio
- Win rate
- Profit/loss by position
- Risk per trade
- Return consistency

The long-term investment objective is to generate approximately 17% annualized net returns while prioritizing capital preservation and consistency over return maximization.

Performance should not be evaluated by return alone. A lower-return outcome with superior drawdown control may be preferable to a higher-return outcome achieved through excessive risk.

## Governance

This policy should be reviewed periodically as Livie Capital evolves. Any material change to investment universe, risk limits, portfolio construction rules, or reporting methodology should be documented before implementation.
