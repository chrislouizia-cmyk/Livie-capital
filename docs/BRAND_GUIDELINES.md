# LIVIE CAPITAL BRAND GUIDELINES

## Brand Mission

Livie Capital exists to protect capital, compound responsibly, and create sustainable growth. The brand must communicate institutional discipline, long-term trust, and access to financial opportunity.

The visual and verbal identity should make people feel that Livie Capital is careful with risk, serious about performance, and committed to building something durable for individuals, families, institutions, and the San Chita community.

## Brand Personality

Livie Capital should feel:

- Premium
- Trustworthy
- Disciplined
- Global
- Quietly confident
- Transparent
- Long-term oriented
- Community aware

The brand should not feel loud, speculative, flashy, or promotional. Confidence should come from clarity and restraint, not hype.

## Voice and Tone

The Livie Capital voice is institutional, calm, and direct. It should sound like a responsible capital steward, not a trading influencer.

Use language that is:

- Clear
- Measured
- Precise
- Educative
- Long-term focused
- Respectful of risk

Avoid:

- Unrealistic return promises
- Emotional trading language
- Excessive urgency
- Vague claims of superiority
- Meme-style finance language
- Overly complex jargon when simple words are stronger

Preferred tone examples:

- "Capital preservation comes first."
- "Risk is defined before capital is deployed."
- "Growth must be earned through consistency."
- "Performance matters, but discipline is mandatory."

## Typography

Livie Capital typography should combine Apple-like clarity with institutional finance precision.

Recommended typography system:

- Primary sans-serif: Geist, Inter, SF Pro, or a similar modern neutral sans-serif
- Monospace: Geist Mono, SF Mono, or a similar financial terminal-style mono font
- Use sans-serif fonts for navigation, headings, reports, and narrative copy
- Use monospace fonts for numbers, prices, tickers, percentages, timestamps, and risk metrics

Typography principles:

- Keep headings confident but restrained
- Avoid oversized marketing typography inside dashboards
- Use tight, readable hierarchy for dense financial information
- Maintain generous spacing around strategic or narrative content
- Keep number formatting consistent across reports and dashboards

## Color Palette

The Livie Capital palette should feel dark, premium, and institutional, with controlled accents for financial signals.

Core colors:

- Deep Black: `#050505`
- Charcoal Panel: `#09090B`
- Zinc Surface: `#18181B`
- Border White 10%: `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.10)`
- Primary Text: `#FFFFFF`
- Secondary Text: `#A1A1AA`
- Muted Text: `#52525B`

Accent colors:

- Capital Green: `#6EE7B7`
- Positive Green: `#34D399`
- Risk Amber: `#FCD34D`
- Loss Red: `#FDA4AF`
- Data Blue: `#7DD3FC`
- Neutral Silver: `#D4D4D8`

Color principles:

- Use dark surfaces as the foundation
- Use green sparingly for positive performance or live status
- Use amber for caution, drawdown, or review states
- Use red only for loss, stop loss, or risk breach
- Avoid decorative gradients, loud color blocks, or casino-style palettes
- Never rely on color alone to communicate risk

## Dashboard Design Principles

The dashboard should feel like an institutional investment platform with Apple-level restraint.

Principles:

- Prioritize clarity over decoration
- Make the first screen operational, not promotional
- Use compact panels for financial information
- Keep dashboard cards sharp, restrained, and consistent
- Avoid nested cards unless displaying repeated items
- Use clear section hierarchy: overview, portfolio, performance, positions, strategy, reports
- Use monospace for all financial values
- Keep interactions predictable and professional
- Surface risk information close to performance information

Dashboard layout should support:

- Fast scanning
- Position monitoring
- Risk review
- Performance review
- Reporting access
- Strategic context

## Report Design Principles

Reports should feel like institutional documents: calm, structured, and audit-friendly.

Report principles:

- Lead with NAV, performance, drawdown, and risk
- Separate realized and unrealized performance
- Include methodology notes where appropriate
- Use consistent date ranges and reporting periods
- Avoid marketing language in performance reporting
- Make risk disclosures visible and understandable
- Prefer tables, concise commentary, and clear summary blocks

Report types:

- Daily NAV brief
- Weekly risk review
- Monthly investor letter
- Annual fund report

Each report should answer:

- What changed?
- Why did it change?
- What is the current risk?
- What decisions were made?
- What requires attention?

## Data Visualization Standards

Data visualization must be honest, readable, and restrained.

Standards:

- Use line charts for equity curve and NAV history
- Use bars for allocation, exposure, and contribution
- Use tables for positions, trades, and reports
- Use green for positive values and red for negative values
- Use amber for caution or threshold proximity
- Keep chart backgrounds dark and clean
- Avoid unnecessary 3D, heavy shadows, or decorative chart effects
- Always label axes, periods, or units when context may be unclear
- Do not manipulate scale to exaggerate performance

Financial visualization priorities:

1. Accuracy
2. Readability
3. Consistency
4. Trust
5. Beauty

## Logo Usage

The Livie Capital logo should communicate permanence, clarity, and disciplined growth.

Logo principles:

- Use the logo on dark backgrounds whenever possible
- Maintain generous clear space around the mark
- Avoid stretching, skewing, rotating, or adding effects
- Do not place the logo over noisy images
- Do not use gradients, glows, or decorative shadows on the logo
- Keep logo usage minimal inside dashboards

Preferred usage:

- Sidebar header
- Report cover
- Investor letters
- Favicon/app icon system
- Presentation title slides

Avoid:

- Repeating the logo excessively
- Using the logo as a background pattern
- Pairing the logo with speculative or promotional language

## Favicon Guidelines

The favicon should be simple, high-contrast, and legible at small sizes.

Recommended favicon direction:

- Minimal monogram or mark
- Dark background
- Light foreground
- Optional restrained green accent
- No small text
- No complex line detail
- No thin strokes that disappear at 16px

Favicon sizes to support:

- 16x16
- 32x32
- 48x48
- 180x180 Apple touch icon
- 192x192 web app icon
- 512x512 web app icon

The favicon should feel like a financial institution, not a consumer app icon or trading signal badge.

## Brand Synthesis

Livie Capital combines three influences:

### Institutional Finance

Discipline, risk management, reporting rigor, and trust.

### Apple Design

Clarity, restraint, precision, and premium simplicity.

### San Chita Community

Access, education, opportunity, and responsibility to the people the firm serves.

The result should be a brand that feels premium but not distant, disciplined but not cold, global but still rooted in community.

## Final Standard

Every brand decision should pass the Livie Capital filter:

- Does it protect trust?
- Does it improve clarity?
- Does it support disciplined growth?

If not, it does not belong.
